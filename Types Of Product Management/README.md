
# Types of Product Management: 

In general, there are around 8–10 major types of Product Management focus area. Below are the major five types, and beyond these, additional variations emerge based on industry, making product management a highly diverse and adaptable discipline.

1. General Product Manager 

2. Technical Product Manager (TPM)

3. Growth Product Manager

4. Enterprise / B2B Product Manager

5. All Product Specific [AI / ML Product Manager, Consumer Product Manager, Platform Product Manager, Data Product Manager]


-------------------------

------------------------

**Detailed Scope:-**

**1. General Product Management [End to end product ownership]**   

            A. Define product vision & strategy
            B. Own roadmap & feature prioritization
            C. Work cross-functionally (engineering, design, marketing)
            D. Track KPIs and business impact
            
      Area of Focus: Companies building customer-facing digital products (apps, SaaS, marketplaces).

**Core Competencies:-**

            A) Problem framing - Turning vague requests into sharp problem statements
            B) Prioritization and tradeoffs - Saying “no” with a reason people accept.
            C) Communication - Writing and speaking clearly across technical + non-technical audiences.
            D) Customer empathy + product sense - Knowing what users value, and what they won’t care about.
            E) Execution reliability - Shipping high-quality work consistently.
            F) Data literacy - Being able to reason with metrics with/without being a data scientist. 

**Example Scenario's For General/Core Product Management**

Example 1 (AI Startup):- At an AI startup building a customer support automation tool, the Core PM notices low AI auto-resolution (32%) and rising churn. Through user interviews and data analysis, they discover hallucinations and outdated knowledge as root causes. They define a clear goal: increase auto-resolution to 50% without reducing CSAT. After exploring options, they prioritize adding Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and a confidence-based escalation system.
They write a PRD with defined scope, risks, and success metrics. During development, they balance latency tradeoffs and timeline pressure. They launch with a beta group before full rollout. Post-launch, resolution improves to 52% and churn decreases—validating the strategy.

Example 2 (Cloud Based SaaS);- A Core PM working on Cloud Backup notices that enterprise customers struggle with complex setup and low first-time configuration success. Data shows only 45% of new customers complete backup configuration within the first week. The PM defines a goal: increase successful setup rate to 70% in one quarter without increasing support tickets.Through customer interviews and telemetry analysis, they identify unclear documentation and poor onboarding flow as root causes. They prioritize a guided setup wizard, automated configuration validation, and clearer error messaging over adding new backup features. The PM aligns engineering, UX, support, and compliance teams across regions. A staged rollout begins with large enterprise clients before global release. After launch, setup success improves to 72% and support tickets drop by 18%, validating the product strategy.

Example 3 (Cloud Service) :- At cloud storage service, a Core PM working on Drive notices that enterprise users are not adopting the new AI-powered document summarization feature. Usage data shows only 18% of eligible users try it, and repeat usage is low. Customer interviews reveal concerns about data privacy and unclear accuracy of summaries. The PM defines a goal: increase weekly active usage of AI summaries by 30% while maintaining enterprise compliance standards. They prioritize improving summary accuracy, adding admin-level privacy controls, and clearer in-product education instead of launching new AI features. The PM coordinates across engineering, UX, security, and sales teams.
A controlled rollout is launched to select Workspace enterprise customers first. Post-launch, adoption increases to 35% and enterprise retention improves, supporting a broader rollout.

**2. Technical Product Management (TPM) [Highly technical systems and infrastructure]**

            A. Work closely with engineers on architecture
            B. Define APIs, platforms, integrations
            C. Translate technical constraints into product decisions
            D. Own backend or platform products

       Area of Focus: B2B SaaS, developer tools, AI/ML platforms, infrastructure companies.

**Example Scenario's For Technical Product Management**

Example 1 (Cloud Infrastructure / Distributed Systems) :- Technical Product Manager working on Cloud infrastructure notices that enterprise customers experience scaling instability during traffic spikes. Monitoring data shows auto-scaling delays under high-load distributed workloads. The TPM sets a goal: improve cluster auto-scaling response time by 30% without increasing compute costs. Instead of expanding hardware capacity, they prioritize improvements in scaling algorithms, workload prediction models, and node provisioning efficiency. The TPM collaborates with site reliability engineers (SREs), distributed systems engineers, and capacity planning teams. Following staged rollouts to enterprise cloud clients, system elasticity improves significantly, reducing downtime incidents and increasing customer retention among large-scale SaaS providers.

Example 2 (Enterprise Cloud & Security Infrastructure):- Technical Product Manager working on identity infrastructure detects rising authentication latency for multinational enterprise customers. Internal telemetry indicates bottlenecks in token validation services across global data centers. The TPM defines a target: reduce global authentication latency by 25% while maintaining enterprise-grade security and compliance standards. Rather than introducing new identity features, they focus on optimizing token caching mechanisms, improving regional load balancing, and strengthening failover systems. The TPM coordinates with distributed systems engineers, security architects, and compliance teams. After incremental global deployment, authentication response times improve by 28%, enhancing enterprise reliability and reinforcing competitive position.

**3. Growth Product Management**

           A. Run A/B experiments
           B. Optimize conversion funnels
           C. Improve onboarding
           D. Drive engagement metrics
      
      Area of Focus: CAC, LTV, retention rate, conversion rate

**4. Enterprise / B2B Product Manager [Build products for businesses]**

            A. Long sales cycles
            B. Stakeholder management
            C. Custom requirements
            D. Compliance & integrations

      Area of Focus: CRM systems, HR software, ERP tools.

5. All Product Specific

      Area of Focus: Consumer electronics, IoT devices, medical devices, Mobile apps, eCommerce platforms, social media.
