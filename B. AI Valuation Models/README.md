
**Valuation Strategies:**

Determining the worth or value of an asset, business, or investment. How much is this worth — and why?”

**Contextand Purpose of Valuation**

A. Investing: To decide if a stock or bond is overpriced or underpriced.

B. Mergers & Acquisitions:	To determine a fair deal price between buyer and seller.

C. Startups & Venture Capital:	To estimate company worth before fundraising.

D. Corporate Finance:	For capital budgeting, restructuring, and reporting.

E. Tax & Legal:	For estate planning, taxation, or litigation.

F. Everyday Life:	Even when buy land, art.

**Valuating AI Models**

1. Cost-Based Valuation (What it costs to build) - Concept: The value equals the total investment required to recreate or replace the model.

      Value = Development Cost + Training Cost + Deployment + Maintenance

2. Market-Based Valuation (What others pay for similar models) - Concept: Compare your AI model to similar assets that have been licensed, acquired, or valued in transactions.

      Value = Benchmark multiple × Comparable performance/usage metric
      (e.g., $ per user, $ per API call, or multiple of revenue)

3. Income-Based Valuation (What it earns) - Concept: The value equals the present value of future economic benefits generated by the AI model.

      Use DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) adapted for AI:

4. Option-Based Valuation (What potential it holds) - Concept: AI models can create strategic options — future opportunities (new features, products, or spin-offs).

5. Data and IP Valuation (What it’s built on) - Concept: Since an AI model’s value depends heavily on data quality and intellectual property, you assess:

      Model Value = (DCF value × 60%) + (Replacement Cost × 20%) + (Option Value × 20%)
