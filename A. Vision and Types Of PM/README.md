
**Vision:** To advance by building a solid knowledge framework, leveraging continuous learning, and making a lasting impact on enterprise growth. The goal here is to build the strong "Foundations Of Knowledge, Growth, Create Value, Drive Strategy, and Empower".


# Types of Product Management: 

In general, there are around 8–10 major types of Product Management focus area. Below are the major five types, and beyond these, additional variations emerge based on industry, making product management a highly diverse and adaptable discipline.

1. General Product Manager 

2. Technical Product Manager (TPM)

3. Growth Product Manager

4. Enterprise / B2B Product Manager

5. All Product Specific [AI / ML Product Manager, Consumer Product Manager, Platform Product Manager, Data Product Manager]

**Detailed Scope:-**

**1. General Product Management [End to end product ownership]**   

            A. Define product vision & strategy
            B. Own roadmap & feature prioritization
            C. Work cross-functionally (engineering, design, marketing)
            D. Track KPIs and business impact
            
      Area of Focus: Companies building customer-facing digital products (apps, SaaS, marketplaces).

**Core Competencies:-**

            A) Problem framing - Turning requests into sharp problem statements
            B) Prioritization and tradeoffs
            C) Communication - Writing and speaking clearly across technical + non-technical audiences.
            D) Customer empathy + product sense - Knowing what users value, and what they won’t care about.
            E) Execution reliability - Shipping high-quality work consistently.
            F) Data literacy - Being able to reason with metrics. 

**Example Scenario's For General/Core Product Management**

Scenario 1 (Med Tech):- At a medical healthcare equipment SaaS provider offering device management software to hospitals, a Core PM notices that only 55% of connected devices are actively monitored, leading to unexpected equipment downtime. Through hospital feedback, they identify onboarding and lack of real time maintenance alerts as root causes. The PM sets a goal to increase active device monitoring to 80% within two quarters. They prioritize automated device health alerts, simplified onboarding, and preventive maintenance dashboards over adding new hardware features. After a phased rollout with key hospital partners, monitoring adoption rises to 83%. Equipment downtime decreases significantly, improving hospital operational efficiency and client retention.

Scenario 2 (AI Startup):- At an AI startup building a customer support automation tool, the Core PM notices low AI auto-resolution (32%) and rising churn. Through user feedback and data analysis, they discover hallucinations and outdated knowledge as root causes. They define a clear goal: increase auto-resolution to 50% without reducing CSAT. After exploring options, they prioritize adding Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and a confidence-based escalation system.
They write a PRD with defined scope, risks, and success metrics. During development, they balance latency tradeoffs and timeline pressure. They launch with a beta group before full rollout. Post-launch, resolution improves to 52% and churn decreases.

Scenario 3 (Cloud Based SaaS);- A Core PM working on Cloud Backup notices that enterprise customers struggle with complex setup and low first-time configuration success. Data shows only 45% of new customers complete backup configuration within the first week. The PM defines a goal: increase successful setup rate to 70% in one quarter without increasing support tickets.Through customer feedback and telemetry analysis, they identify unclear documentation and other flows as root causes. They prioritize a guided setup wizard, automated configuration validation, and clearer error messaging over adding new backup features. The PM aligns engineering, UX, support, and compliance teams across regions. A staged rollout begins with large enterprise clients before global release. After launch, setup success improves to 72% and support tickets drop by 18%.

Scenario 4 (Cloud Service) :- At cloud storage service, a Core PM working on Drive notices that enterprise users are not adopting the new AI-powered document summarization feature. Usage data shows only 18% of eligible users try it, and repeat usage is low. Customer feedback reveal concerns about data privacy and unclear accuracy of summaries. The PM defines a goal: increase weekly active usage of AI summaries by 30% while maintaining enterprise compliance standards. They prioritize improving summary accuracy, adding admin-level privacy controls, and clearer in-product education instead of launching new AI features. The PM coordinates across engineering, UX, security, and sales teams.
A controlled rollout is launched to select Workspace enterprise customers first. Post-launch, adoption increases to 35% and enterprise retention improves, supporting a broader rollout.

**2. Technical Product Management (TPM) [Highly technical systems and infrastructure]**

            A. Work closely with engineers on architecture
            B. Define APIs, platforms, integrations
            C. Translate technical constraints into product decisions
            D. Own backend or platform products

       Area of Focus: B2B SaaS, developer tools, AI/ML platforms, infrastructure companies.

**Example Scenario's For Technical Product Management**

Scenario 1 (MedTech Technical Infrastructure):- A Technical Product Manager at a MedTech company managing a cloud platform for connected devices notices delayed real-time data synchronization during peak hospital usage hours. Monitoring shows latency spikes in device-to-cloud APIs, impacting live patient monitoring dashboards. The TPM sets a goal: reduce data transmission latency by 40% without increasing infrastructure costs or compromising regulatory compliance (HIPAA). They prioritize optimizing edge processing, improving API throughput, and redesigning the messaging queue architecture. The TPM works closely with embedded engineers, cloud infrastructure teams, cybersecurity, and compliance stakeholders. After phased validation in partner hospitals, latency drops by 45%, improving real-time reliability and strengthening enterprise hospital contracts.

Scenario 2 (Cloud Infrastructure / Distributed Systems) :- Technical Product Manager working on Cloud infrastructure notices that enterprise customers experience scaling instability during traffic spikes. Monitoring data shows auto-scaling delays under high-load distributed workloads. The TPM sets a goal: improve cluster auto-scaling response time by 30% without increasing compute costs. Instead of expanding hardware capacity, they prioritize improvements in scaling algorithms, workload prediction models, and node provisioning efficiency. The TPM collaborates with site reliability engineers (SREs), distributed systems engineers, and capacity planning teams. Following staged rollouts to enterprise cloud clients, system elasticity improves significantly, reducing downtime incidents and increasing customer retention among large-scale SaaS providers.

Scenario 3 (Enterprise Cloud & Security Infrastructure):- Technical Product Manager working on identity infrastructure detects rising authentication latency for multinational enterprise customers. Internal telemetry indicates bottlenecks in token validation services across global data centers. The TPM defines a target: reduce global authentication latency by 25% while maintaining enterprise-grade security and compliance standards. Rather than introducing new identity features, they focus on optimizing token caching mechanisms, improving regional load balancing, and strengthening failover systems. The TPM coordinates with distributed systems engineers, security architects, and compliance teams. After incremental global deployment, authentication response times improve by 28%, enhancing enterprise reliability and reinforcing competitive position.

**3. Growth Product Management**

           A. Run A/B experiments
           B. Optimize conversion funnels
           C. Improve onboarding
           D. Drive engagement metrics
      
      Area of Focus: CAC, LTV, retention rate, conversion rate

Scenario 1 (Growth - Consoles):- A Growth Product Manager at a MedTech company providing imaging consoles notices that only 40% of radiologist actively use advanced visualization features after installation. Funnel analysis shows drop-offs during initial console setup and low repeat usage of premium modules. The PM sets a goal to increase feature adoption to 65% and improve 90-day retention across hospital accounts. They run A/B tests on guided in-console tutorials, optimize onboarding workflows for OR staff, and introduce contextual prompts highlighting feature benefits during procedures. After phased rollouts and usage cohort tracking, advanced feature adoption rises to 68%, and hospital renewal rates improve, increasing LTV across enterprise accounts.

LTV - Lifetime Value [Average Revenue per Customer per Period ÷ Churn Rate]

LTV=ARPU×Customer Lifetime

If a hospital/customer pays: $50,000 per year for a console software subscription, And stays for 5 years.

Then: LTV = $50,000 × 5 = $250,000, That means one hospital account is worth $250K over its lifetime.

A healthy SaaS business usually aims for: LTV:CAC≥3:1 (which means for every $1 you spend to acquire a customer, you should earn at least $3 from that customer over their lifetime.)

Break it down: LTV (Lifetime Value) = Total revenue you expect from a customer over the entire relationship, CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) = Total cost to acquire that customer (sales, marketing, commissions, ads, etc.)

LTV Helps on:-

            A. Evaluate Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
            B. Determines profitability
            C. Guides pricing decisions
            D. Influences retention and upsell strategy
